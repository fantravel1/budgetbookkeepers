# Creative Agencies: Financial Management Guide

## Industry Overview

Creative agencies (design, marketing, web development, advertising) have unique characteristics:
- **Project-based** (client engagements vary in scope/size)
- **Time-tracking intensive** (hours billable to clients)
- **Multiple deliverables** (design, development, copywriting, strategy)
- **Talent-heavy** (staff is your product)
- **Retainer + project mix** (some recurring, some one-time)
- **High overhead** (creative talent expensive)

---

## Business Models

### Time & Materials
Bill for hours spent + materials
- Variable revenue
- Requires accurate time tracking
- Can expand project scope (risky)

### Fixed Project Price
Quote for entire project, deliver agreed scope
- Fixed revenue (can't increase)
- Risk of scope creep
- Requires accurate estimation

### Retainer Model
Monthly fee for ongoing services
- Predictable revenue
- Capped hours (must manage scope)
- Better cash flow

### Hybrid
Mix of retainer + project work
- Most sustainable
- Retainer provides base, projects drive growth
- Better for cash flow

## Key Metrics

### Billable Utilization Rate
Hours billed ÷ Total hours worked

**Example**:
- Hours worked: 2,000
- Hours billed: 1,600
- Utilization: 80%

**Target**: 70-80% (need 20-30% for admin, sales, learning)

### Effective Hourly Rate
Total revenue ÷ Total hours worked

**Example**:
- Revenue: $200,000
- Hours: 2,000
- Effective rate: $100/hour

Your card rate might be $150/hour, but effective rate is $100/hour.

### Utilization per Project
How much time spent vs. hours billed

**Example**:
- Website project quote: 100 hours @ $150 = $15,000
- Actual time: 120 hours
- Over by 20 hours (underbilled due to scope creep)

Identify projects running over systematically.

### Client Profitability
Some clients are profitable, some lose money

**Example**:
- Client A: $50,000 revenue, $30,000 cost = $20,000 profit (40% margin)
- Client B: $30,000 revenue, $28,000 cost = $2,000 profit (7% margin)
- Client C: $20,000 revenue, $25,000 cost = -$5,000 LOSS

Identify and address unprofitable clients.

## Accounting Challenges

### 1. Time Tracking & Allocation

Critical for project profitability:

**Example Project**:
- Quoting: 5 hours (research, proposal)
- Design: 30 hours
- Revisions: 10 hours (scope creep)
- Development: 40 hours
- Client meetings: 10 hours
- Total: 95 hours

Each task must be tracked to understand profitability.

### 2. Scope Creep

Biggest profit killer in agency work:

**Example**:
- Quoted scope: "5-page website"
- Client adds: "Oh, and can you add a blog? And integrate our email?"
- Hours balloon from 40 to 60
- Margin shrinks from 50% to 33%

Prevent with: Detailed scope document, change order process.

### 3. Client Retainer Accounting

Monthly retainer should specify deliverables:

**Example**:
- Retainer: $5,000/month
- Includes: 40 hours of work (social media, content, strategy)
- If hours exceed 40: Either cover from retainer or bill extra

Track hours used against retainer to know profitability.

### 4. Contractor Costs

Many agencies use contractors:

**Example**:
- Client work billed: $30,000
- Contractor payment: $15,000 (50% cost)
- Gross margin: 50%
- But need to cover overhead

Track contractor costs per project.

## Pricing Strategies

### Hourly Billing
- Simple to implement
- Doesn't incentivize efficiency
- Scales poorly (limited hours)
- Range: $75-300+/hour depending on specialization

### Fixed Project Pricing
- Better margin if efficient
- Risk if estimation is wrong
- Must overestimate to account for unknowns
- Industry standard is 50% margin

### Value-Based Pricing
- Based on value to client
- Highest profit potential
- "This SEO work will add $100k revenue to your business"
- Hardest to justify but most profitable

## Common Mistakes

### ❌ Not Tracking Time
Can't tell profitability without it.

### ❌ Allowing Unlimited Revisions
"One more round of revisions" doesn't seem like much but adds 10+ hours.

### ❌ Underestimating Projects
Conservative estimates good, but too conservative = losses.

### ❌ Not Raising Rates
Rates should increase 10-15% annually minimum. Client acquisition inflation is real.

### ❌ Ignoring Unprofitable Clients
Some clients take more energy, yield less profit. Fire them (diplomatically).

## Financial Management

**Critical Daily**:
- Time entry (log hours to projects daily, not weekly)
- Project status (on track or over budget?)

**Critical Weekly**:
- Project hours vs. budget
- Scope change requests (log them as change orders)
- Billable rate variance

**Critical Monthly**:
- Client profitability
- Utilization rate
- Project margin analysis
- Identify over/under budget projects
- Contractor costs

## Agency Financial Wisdom

**The metric that matters most**: **Project margin**

Two agencies both billing $200k/year:
- Agency A: 40% margin = $80k profit
- Agency B: 20% margin = $40k profit

Difference? Project selection, scope management, pricing discipline.

Master these, and your agency becomes sustainable and scalable.
