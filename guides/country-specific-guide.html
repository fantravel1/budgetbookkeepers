Key Tax Requirements

  • GST/HST Registration: Required if annual revenue exceeds CAD $30,000. You charge GST/HST on services and can claim it on business expenses.
  • Provincial Sales Tax: Varies by province (Ontario has HST, Quebec has QST, etc.)
  • Self-Employment Tax: If you're self-employed, you pay both employer and employee CPP contributions
  • Reporting Deadline: June 15 for tax filing (but payment due April 30)
  • Installments: If you owe more than CAD $3,000, you may need to make quarterly installment payments

Payroll Considerations

  • If you have employees, you must deduct income tax, CPP, and EI from paychecks
  • Employer contributions required for CPP and EI
  • Provincial workers' compensation insurance may be required
  • Remittance deadlines vary (monthly or quarterly depending on payroll size)

Record Retention

Keep all records for 6 years (CRA audit standard). Digital records are acceptable if they're legible and searchable.

Currency

Use Canadian dollars. If you have USD income, you must convert at the Bank of Canada exchange rate on the transaction date.

Recommended: Find a bookkeeper experienced with Canadian GST/HST compliance and provincial variations. Many Canadian bookkeepers partner with small business accountants for seamless tax prep.

ðŸ‡ºðŸ‡¸ United States

Key Tax Requirements

  • Sales Tax: Varies dramatically by state (0-10%+). Some states have no sales tax; others require sales tax on services too.
  • Self-Employment Tax: Social Security + Medicare taxes (15.3% total) paid on business profit
  • Quarterly Estimated Taxes: Due April 15, June 15, Sept 15, and Jan 15
  • State Income Tax: Varies by state. Some states have no income tax; others do.
  • Federal Tax Deadline: April 15 annually

Payroll Considerations

  • Federal and state income tax withholding required
  • Social Security and Medicare (FICA) withholding
  • State unemployment insurance (varies by state)
  • Workers' compensation insurance (required in most states)
  • Pay frequency: vary by state (but most allow bi-weekly)

Business Structure Matters

Your business structure (sole proprietor, LLC, S-Corp, C-Corp) significantly affects taxes. This is where a good bookkeeper plus CPA consultation is critical.

Currency

Use US dollars. If you have income in other currencies, convert using the exchange rate on the transaction date.

Recommended: Find a US bookkeeper who understands your specific state's requirements. Sales tax handling varies so widely that state-specific expertise is valuable.

  • PAYE (Pay As You Earn) withholding required if you have employees
  • Employer National Insurance contributions required
  • Payroll must be reported in real-time to HMRC (RTI system)
  • Auto-enrolment pension scheme required once you have employees

Currency

Use British pounds. If you have euro or USD income, use the exchange rate on the transaction date. Keep records of the rate used.

Recommended: Find a UK bookkeeper with VAT experience. VAT compliance is a significant responsibility and mistakes can trigger HMRC inquiries.

ðŸ‡¦ðŸ‡º Australia

Key Tax Requirements

  • ABN (Australian Business Number): Required if turnover exceeds AUD $75,000. Apply through ASIC.
  • GST (Goods & Services Tax): 10% on most services. Must register if turnover exceeds AUD $75,000 (or choose to voluntarily register).
  • Income Tax: Ranges from 19% to 47% depending on income level. Filing deadline: October 31.
  • Super (Superannuation): Employers must contribute 11.5% to employee retirement accounts.
  • PAYG Withholding: Tax withheld from employee paychecks and remitted monthly

Record Keeping

The ATO (Australian Tax Office) requires records for 5 years. Digital records are accepted.

MYOB vs Xero

MYOB and Xero are both popular in Australia. Some older bookkeepers prefer MYOB; most modern bookkeepers use Xero.

Currency

Use Australian dollars. If you have income in other currencies, conversion is required with documentation of the rate used.

Recommended: Find a bookkeeper with CPA (Australia) designation or AAT certification. GST compliance and PAYG understanding are critical.

Universal Bookkeeping Principles (Regardless of Country)

  • Separate Accounts: Keep business and personal finances completely separate
  • Document Everything: Keep receipts, invoices, and documentation for every transaction
  • Consistent Records: Record transactions regularly (daily or weekly, not annually)
  • Reconciliation: Reconcile bank accounts monthly
  • Professional Advice: Work with a local accountant familiar with your country's tax laws
  • Regular Reviews: Understand your financial position monthly, not just at tax time
