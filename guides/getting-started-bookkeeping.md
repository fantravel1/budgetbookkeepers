# Getting Started with Bookkeeping: A Complete Guide for Women Entrepreneurs

## Introduction

Whether you're running an e-commerce store, freelance service, or small business, bookkeeping is the foundation of financial success. This guide walks you through everything you need to know to get started, even if you've never done bookkeeping before.

**Time to read: 12 minutes**

---

## What is Bookkeeping vs. Accounting?

### Bookkeeping
Bookkeeping is the **daily recording** of financial transactions:
- Invoice customers
- Record expenses
- Track payments
- Reconcile bank statements
- Categorize transactions
- Generate monthly reports

Think of it as keeping a financial diary of your business.

### Accounting
Accounting uses bookkeeping data to:
- Prepare tax returns
- Provide financial insights
- Plan for growth
- Meet regulatory requirements
- Make strategic decisions

**Key Takeaway**: Bookkeeping is data entry + organization. Accounting is analysis + strategy.

## The Bookkeeping Basics

### Five Core Tasks

#### 1. **Track Income**
Every time you earn money:
- Record the date
- Record the amount
- Note the customer/source
- Categorize by service/product type

**Example**: "Feb 15, Invoice #201 to Sarah's Salon, $500 for web design, paid via bank transfer"

#### 2. **Track Expenses**
Every time you spend money:
- Record the date and amount
- Keep receipts
- Categorize logically
- Note the business purpose

**Example**: "Feb 12, Adobe Creative Cloud subscription, $54.99, software expense"

**Common Expense Categories**:
- Software & subscriptions
- Office supplies
- Equipment
- Marketing & advertising
- Professional fees (accountant, lawyer)
- Travel & mileage
- Utilities
- Inventory
- Freelance contractors

#### 3. **Categorize Transactions**
Group income and expenses by type so you can see:
- What brings in the most revenue?
- What costs the most?
- What's actually profitable?

**Smart Categories**:
- Keep it simple (8-15 categories initially)
- Match your business type
- Be consistent
- Adjust annually

#### 4. **Reconcile Bank Accounts**
Monthly, match your records to your bank statement:
- Bank shows $5,000 in deposits
- Your records show $5,000 in income
- ✅ They match = you're on track
- ❌ They don't match = something's wrong

This catches errors, fraud, and mistakes early.

#### 5. **Generate Reports**
Monthly, create simple reports to understand:
- **Profit & Loss**: Revenue minus expenses
- **Cash Position**: Money in bank right now
- **Expenses Breakdown**: Where money is going
- **Income by Type**: Which products/services earn most

## Setting Up Your Bookkeeping System

### Step 1: Choose Your Method

#### Option A: Spreadsheet (Free, DIY)
**Best for**: Solo freelancers, very simple businesses
- Use Excel or Google Sheets
- Create simple templates
- Record transactions monthly
- **Pros**: Free, simple, full control
- **Cons**: Easy to make mistakes, time-consuming

**Time commitment**: 5-10 hours/month

#### Option B: Bookkeeping Software ($10-50/month)
**Best for**: Growing businesses, multiple income streams
- Xero, QuickBooks, Wave, FreshBooks
- Automated bank connections
- Professional reports
- Tax prep support
- **Pros**: Automatic, accurate, integration with banks
- **Cons**: Cost, learning curve

**Time commitment**: 2-5 hours/month

#### Option C: Professional Bookkeeper ($150-1,000/month)
**Best for**: Complex businesses, multiple locations, team management
- Hire a bookkeeper or accountant
- They handle all daily transactions
- You get monthly reports
- **Pros**: Expert quality, saves time, accuracy
- **Cons**: Recurring cost

**Time commitment**: 1-2 hours/month reviewing reports

### Step 2: Set Up Your Chart of Accounts

This is your category list. Example for a freelance designer:

**Income**:
- Web Design Services
- Graphic Design Services
- Consulting

**Expenses**:
- Software & Subscriptions
- Marketing
- Office Supplies
- Equipment
- Professional Development
- Internet & Utilities
- Contractor Payments

**Rule**: Create accounts that match how you think about your business.

### Step 3: Create Your Transaction Recording System

**Daily or Weekly** (whichever works):
1. Save all receipts (digital or physical)
2. Record in your system (software or spreadsheet)
3. Include: Date, amount, description, category
4. Attach receipt for expenses over $25

**Pro Tip**: Set a specific day each week (Tuesday morning?) to catch up on transactions.

### Step 4: Monthly Reconciliation Routine

**First Friday of every month** (or your chosen day):
1. Download bank statement
2. Check all deposits match your income records
3. Check all withdrawals match your expense records
4. Record any bank fees or interest
5. Investigate any differences
6. Generate profit & loss report

**Time needed**: 30-60 minutes

## Common Bookkeeping Mistakes (and How to Avoid Them)

### ❌ Mistake 1: Mixing Personal and Business Money
**The Problem**: Can't tell what's business vs. personal
**The Solution**:
- Open a business bank account
- Use it ONLY for business
- When you need personal money, formally pay yourself

### ❌ Mistake 2: Forgetting to Save Receipts
**The Problem**: Can't prove your expenses to the IRS
**The Solution**:
- Create a "receipts" folder (physical or digital)
- Photograph receipts immediately
- Store digitally (phone photo + backup)

### ❌ Mistake 3: Recording Transactions Months Later
**The Problem**: You forget details, dates are wrong, easy to miss things
**The Solution**:
- Record weekly, not yearly
- Use software with phone/web access
- Make it a habit

### ❌ Mistake 4: Not Categorizing Correctly
**The Problem**: Can't answer "where is my money going?"
**The Solution**:
- Spend 30 minutes creating your category list
- Write definitions (what goes in "Marketing" vs. "Advertising"?)
- Be consistent

### ❌ Mistake 5: Ignoring Bank Reconciliation
**The Problem**: Don't catch fraud, errors, or mistakes
**The Solution**:
- Do it monthly, no excuses
- Investigate any differences immediately
- It only takes 30-60 minutes

## Key Bookkeeping Terminology

| Term | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Income/Revenue** | Money coming in | Selling a product, providing a service |
| **Expense** | Money going out | Supplies, software, salaries |
| **Profit** | Revenue minus expenses | Making $10k, spending $3k = $7k profit |
| **Cash Flow** | Movement of money in/out | Customer pays in 30 days (timing matters!) |
| **Invoice** | Bill sent to customer | #201 for $500 due Feb 28 |
| **Receipt** | Proof of expense | Proving you spent $50 on supplies |
| **Chart of Accounts** | Your category list | Your expense/income buckets |
| **Bank Reconciliation** | Matching your records to bank | Proving your numbers are accurate |
| **Profit & Loss (P&L)** | Your monthly/yearly summary | Total income - Total expenses |

## Red Flags: When to Get Professional Help

Consider hiring a professional bookkeeper if:
- You're spending more than 5 hours/week on bookkeeping
- You have multiple revenue streams
- You have employees
- You operate in multiple states/countries
- Your business revenue exceeds $100,000/year
- You feel overwhelmed or uncertain

**Good News**: Professional bookkeeping typically costs $200-500/month and saves you 10+ hours/week. The ROI is usually excellent.
