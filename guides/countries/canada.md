# Canada: Comprehensive Tax & Business Guide

## Tax System Overview

### Federal Tax Brackets (2024)
- 15%: $0-55,867
- 20.5%: $55,868-111,733
- 26%: $111,734-173,205
- 29%: $173,206-246,752
- 33%: $246,753+

**Plus provincial tax** (varies: 5-16%+ depending on province)

### Capital Gains
- 50% of capital gains included in income (advantageous)
- Taxed at marginal rate
- Example: $100k gain → $50k inclusion → taxed at personal rate (15-53%)

## Business Structures

### Sole Proprietorship
- No filing required
- Income taxed at personal rate (15-53%)
- No liability protection

### Corporation
- Federal or provincial incorporation (~$500-1,500)
- Small business deduction available (reduced rate ~13.2% on first $500k)
- Liability protection
- More compliance required

**Tax advantage**:
- Profit taxed at ~13.2% in corp (vs. 30-43% personal rate)
- Significant savings if keeping profits in corporation
- Cost of incorporation (~$500-1,500) quickly paid for

### Partnership
- Two or more people
- Each partner reports share of income
- Pass-through entity (not taxed at entity level)

### Cooperative
- Member-owned
- Special tax treatment
- Less common for small business

## Key Deductions

### Universal Deductions
- Home office (workspace in home can deduct %)
- Vehicle (mileage for business use)
- Office supplies
- Software and subscriptions
- Meals (50% deductible)
- Travel (business only)
- Education (business-related)
- Insurance
- Professional fees

### Capital Cost Allowance (CCA - Depreciation)
- Equipment: 20-40% declining balance (varies)
- Building: 4% (very slow)
- Vehicles: 15-30%
- Can claim partial year of acquisition

---

## GST/HST (Sales Tax)

**GST**: 5% federal (all provinces)
**HST** (Harmonized): GST + provincial (13-15% in ON, NS, NL, NB)
**PST**: Provincial sales tax (varies by province)

### GST/HST Registration
- Required if revenue >$30,000 in 4 consecutive quarters
- Can register voluntarily below threshold
- Monthly or quarterly filing (varies by revenue)

### Input Tax Credits
- If registered, can claim GST/HST paid on business expenses
- Recover tax, so net cost of supplies is lower
- Benefit of registration if input costs significant

## Corporate Tax Planning

### Small Business Deduction
- First $500,000 of active income taxed at ~13.2% (federal + provincial average)
- **vs. personal rate of 30-53%**
- **Savings**: $85,000-200,000 on $500k profit

### Dividend Concept
- Corporation can pay dividends to shareholder (you)
- Dividend tax credit available (integrates corporate + personal tax)
- More favorable than salary in many cases

### Salary vs. Dividend Optimization
- Salary: Deductible by corp, taxed to you
- Dividend: Paid from after-tax profit, has tax credit
- Best approach depends on your personal rate vs. corporate rate

## Compliance & Forms

### Corporate Tax Return (T2)
- Due: 6 months after year-end
- If June 30 year-end: Due December 31

### Personal Tax Return (T1)
- Due: June 15 (vs. April 15 USA)
- If self-employed: More time to file

### Payroll Deductions
- If paying employees or dividends: Monthly remittance
- Withhold income tax, CPP, EI
- Due 15th of following month

### GST/HST Return
- Monthly/quarterly depending on revenue
- Claim input tax credits
- Remit or claim refund

## Unique Canadian Benefits

### Registered Retirement Savings Plan (RRSP)
- Contribution limit: 18% of prior year income, max ~$31,560 (2024)
- Deductible (reduces taxable income)
- Tax-deferred growth
- Withdrawal penalized (withholding tax)

### Home Office Deduction (Simplified)
- Canada has two methods:
  - Percentage method: % of home expenses (rent, utilities, insurance)
  - Flat rate: $5/sq ft (max 300 sq ft)
- More generous than USA

### Capital Gains Exemption
- $1.016M lifetime exemption on qualifying small business shares (2024)
- $0 tax on gains up to exemption
- Plan life planning around this

## Provincial Considerations

### High-Tax Provinces (Ontario, BC)
- Combined rate 43-53.5%
- Consider incorporating for small business deduction benefit

### Low-Tax Provinces (AB)
- No provincial sales tax (PST)
- Lower corporate rates
- Many businesses incorporated in AB for this reason

### Multi-Province Operations
- Can have issues with source of income (which province taxes?)
- Plan structure accordingly

## Common Mistakes

### ❌ Not Registering for GST/HST
- Forgoing input tax credits ($000s in savings)
- Register voluntarily if $20-30k revenue

### ❌ Not Using Small Business Deduction
- Sole proprietor paying full personal rate (30-53%)
- Incorporate to access ~13.2% rate
- Often saves $80,000-200,000 on $500k profit

### ❌ Ignoring CPP Contributions
- Self-employed pay both portions (~11%)
- Can deduct employee portion (50%)
- Must track for retirement

### ❌ Poor Mileage Documentation
- Can't claim vehicle deduction without log
- Keep detailed mileage records

### ❌ Missing Meal Deductions
- Only 50% deductible
- Must be for business purpose (client meal, away on business)

## Financial Management

### Estimated Tax Installments
- Not required if tax <$3,000 from self-employment
- If required: Quarterly installments (Mar 15, Jun 15, Sep 15, Dec 15)

### Year-End Deadline (Varies)
- December 31 (most common for corporations)
- Can choose June 30 or other date if structured properly

### Monthly Accounting
- Reconcile accounts
- Track GST/HST
- Monitor payroll if applicable
