# Germany: Tax & Business Guide

## Tax System Overview

### Income Tax Brackets (2024)
- 0%: €0-11,604 (basic exemption)
- 18-42%: Progressive from €11,605-€66,760
- Solidarity surcharge: 5.5% on income tax over €972
- Church tax: 8-9% (if member)

### Capital Gains
- Taxed as ordinary income at marginal rate (up to 45%)
- 50% exemption on gains if held >1 year and <10% ownership

---

## Business Structures

### Sole Trader (Einzelunternehmer)
- Simplest structure
- Income taxed at personal rates (18-45%)
- Unlimited personal liability
- Register with Finanzamt (tax office)

**Trade tax**: 10% (varies by municipality, plus surcharges)

### GmbH (Limited Liability Company)
- Most common small business structure
- Corporate tax: 30% (15% + 5.5% solidarity surcharge)
- Trade tax: ~14% (varies)
- Limited liability
- Must file annual accounts

### UG (Unternehmergesellschaft)
- "Mini GmbH" (lower capital requirement)
- Same tax treatment as GmbH
- Must retain 25% of profits until €25,000 equity reached

## Key Deductions

### Universal Expenses
- Office rent or home office
- Utilities
- Office supplies
- Professional services (accounting, legal)
- Vehicle expenses
- Travel for business
- Marketing
- Insurance
- Equipment (depreciated)
- Software

### Home Office
- Can claim rent %, utilities %, insurance %
- Or €5/month for dedicated office use (simplified)

---

## VAT (MwSt)

**Standard rate**: 19%
**Reduced rate**: 7% (food, books, etc.)

### VAT Registration (Umsatzsteuer)
- Threshold: €22,000 (old) or €50,000 (new from 2024)
- Must register if exceed
- Can register voluntarily

### VAT Returns
- Monthly or quarterly (varies)
- Due 10th of following month (or 15th if paid electronically)

---

## Payroll (Lohnsteuer)

**If paying employees**:
- Income tax withholding (~18-45%)
- Social security: ~42% (employer + employee)
- Pension insurance: ~18.6%
- Health insurance: ~14.6%
- Unemployment: ~2.6%

**Total cost**: Employee salary + ~42% for employer contributions

---

## Depreciation (Abschreibung)

### Linear Depreciation
- Standard: Useful life divided into annual depreciation
- Equipment: 10-13% per year
- Building: 2% per year
- Vehicles: 20% per year

### Accelerated Depreciation
- Possible for certain assets
- Proportional month of acquisition/disposal

---

## Compliance & Deadlines

### Income Tax Return (Einkommensteuererklarung)
- Due: May 31 following year (if using tax advisor: Sept 30)

### Corporate Return (Körperschaftsteuer)
- If GmbH: Due 5 months after year-end
- Dec 31 year-end: Due May 31

### VAT Return (Umsatzsteuererklarung)
- Monthly or quarterly
- Due 10th (or 15th if electronic) following period

### Trade Tax Return (Gewerbesteuererklarung)
- Due 5 months after year-end
- For trade businesses

### Wage Tax Return (Lohnsteuererklarung)
- If employees: Due 10th of month following quarter

---

## Social Security (Sozialversicherung)

### Mandatory for Employees
- Pension: ~9.3% (employer) + ~9.3% (employee)
- Health: ~7.3% (employer) + ~7.3% (employee)
- Unemployment: ~1.3% (employer) + ~1.3% (employee)
- Care insurance: Varies (employer + employee)

### Self-Employed
- Can opt out in some cases
- Usually must participate in pension
- Voluntary health insurance options

---

## Business Licenses & Setup

### Gewerbeanmeldung (Trade Registration)
- Required for most business activities
- Cost: €15-60 depending on municipality
- File with local trade office (Gewerbeamt)

### Tax Number (Steuernummer)
- Issued by Finanzamt upon registration
- Required for all tax purposes

### VAT ID Number (Umsatzsteuer-Identifikationsnummer)
- Required if selling across EU
- Needed for VAT-free transactions
- Different from tax number

---

## Common Mistakes

### ❌ Not Registering for VAT
- Forgoing input tax credits
- Compliance risk
- Register if >threshold, even voluntarily beneficial

### ❌ Ignoring Social Security Obligations
- Costly for employees (employers must pay)
- Non-compliance = severe penalties
- Proper calculation and payment essential

### ❌ Poor Documentation (Belege)
- Must keep receipts 6-10 years
- Digital copies must be legible
- Tax office can disallow deductions for poor docs

### ❌ Not Maximizing Entity Deductions
- GmbH can deduct more than sole trader
- Salary planning important
- Profit splitting between salary and dividends

## Germany Financial Wisdom

**GmbH becomes advantageous at higher profits due to:**
- Trade tax deductibility in corporate taxes
- Flexibility in profit distribution (salary vs. dividend)
- Professional image (good for B2B)

**Structure decision depends on projected profit** (usually >€60,000 profit, GmbH advantageous).
