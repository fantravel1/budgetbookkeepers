# Singapore: Tax & Business Guide

## Tax System Overview

### Corporate Income Tax
- Rate: 17% (one of lowest globally)
- First $75,000: Can be 50% taxable = 8.5% effective
- Exemptions: First $100k of chargeable income (qualifying companies)

### Personal Income Tax (if you're employee)
- 0%: $0-20,000
- 2%: $20,001-30,000
- 3.5%: $30,001-40,000
- Progressive up to 22% (>$500k)
- Can claim business deductions

---

## Business Structures

### Sole Proprietorship
- Simplest structure
- Income taxed at personal rates (0-22%)
- Register with ACRA (Accounting & Corporate Regulatory Authority)
- Unlimited personal liability

**Advantage**: Low tax if income low; sole prop can claim business deductions

### Private Limited Company (Pte Ltd)
- Most common for serious businesses
- Corporate tax: 17% (or 8.5% on first $75k)
- Limited liability
- More compliance (annual reports, financial statements)

**Advantage**: Professional image, investor-friendly, limited liability

### Limited Liability Partnership (LLP)
- Partnership with liability protection
- Taxed as partnership (pass-through)
- Flexibility in structure

---

## Key Deductions

### Universal Expenses
- Office rent (or home office %)
- Utilities
- Office supplies
- Professional services (accounting, legal)
- Vehicle expenses
- Travel for business
- Marketing and advertising
- Insurance
- Equipment (depreciated 5-40% depending on type)
- Software

### Home Office
- Can deduct % of rent, utilities, insurance if dedicated space

---

## GST (Goods & Services Tax)

**Standard rate**: 9% (increased from 7% on Jan 1, 2023)

### GST Registration
- Threshold: SGD $1 million annual turnover
- Must register if exceed
- Can register voluntarily below

### GST Returns
- Generally quarterly (or monthly for large traders)
- Input tax credits available

---

## Depreciation (Capital Allowances)

### Initial Allowance (IA)
- Certain machinery/equipment: 100% year 1
- Accelerates deductions

### Annual Allowance
- Plant & machinery: 10-40% per year (varies)
- Building: 3% per year (industrial buildings get 5%)
- Motor vehicles: 20% per year

---

## Unique Singapore Benefits

### Pioneer Certificate
- Tax exemption or reduction for pioneer firms (high-tech, R&D)
- Up to 10 years
- Requires government approval

### Research & Development Deduction
- R&D expenses: Deductible
- Government grants/incentives available
- Attracts tech businesses

### Development Grants & Credits
- Various incentives for innovation, sustainability
- Can offset taxes due

### Approved Headquarters Status
- HQ of regional operations: Tax incentives
- Good for multinational businesses

---

## Employee-Related Taxes

### Central Provident Fund (CPF)
- Mandatory employee savings
- Employer contribution: 15-17% (varies by age)
- Employee: 7-11% (varies by age)
- Generous compared to other countries

### Foreign Worker Levy
- If hiring foreigners: Pay levy (month)
- Encourages local hiring

---

## Compliance & Deadlines

### Corporate Income Tax Return
- Due: 5 months after year-end
- Dec 31 year-end: Due May 31
- Extension available (8 months)

### Annual Report & Financial Statements
- Listed company: 4 months after year-end
- Private company: 5 months after year-end
- Must lodge with ACRA

### GST Return
- Quarterly: Due 1 month after end of quarter

### Payroll (CPF Contribution)
- Monthly: Due by 4th of following month

---

## Common Mistakes

### ❌ Not Claiming All Deductions
- Many expat businesses leave money on table
- Claim home office, vehicle, travel, professional services

### ❌ Ignoring GST Threshold
- Reach SGD $1M turnover, forget to register
- Backdated registration required

### ❌ Poor Dividend Planning
- Dividend tax credit system complex
- Plan timing and amount of distributions

### ❌ Not Using Pioneer Certificate
- Tech/R&D businesses should apply
- Can save 5-10 years of taxes

---

## Financial Management

### Monthly Bookkeeping
- Record all transactions
- GST tracking for registration
- CPF tracking if employees

### Year-End (Typically Dec 31)
- Complete accounting
- Depreciation schedule
- Annual review

**Singapore is tax-friendly for:**
1. **Tech startups**: R&D deductions, grants, pioneer status
2. **Regional HQ**: Tax incentives, professional infrastructure
3. **Digital businesses**: Low corporate tax (17%), GST 9%
4. **Investors**: Low personal tax, favorable capital gains treatment
