# United Kingdom: Tax & Business Guide

## Tax System Overview

### Income Tax Brackets (2024)
- 20%: £0-50,270
- 40%: £50,271-125,140
- 45%: £125,141+
- No tax: First £1,000 (trading allowance)

### Capital Gains
- 10%: Lower rate (if annual gains <£3,000 or certain conditions)
- 20%: Normal rate
- Annual exemption: £3,000 (no tax on first £3,000 gains)

---

## Business Structures

### Sole Trader
- Register as self-employed
- Income taxed at personal rates (20-45%)
- Unlimited personal liability
- Simple administrative burden

### Limited Company
- File Companies House documents (~£12-150)
- Corporation tax: 19% (reduced from 25% with small profit rate)
- Limited liability (personal protected)
- More compliance (accounts filing, audits)

**Tax comparison**:
- Sole trader profit £100k: ~£40-45k tax
- Ltd company profit £100k: ~£19k corporate tax + personal dividend tax (~£23k) = similar
- Benefit: Limited liability, can retain earnings, more professional

---

## Key Deductions (Allowable Expenses)

### Universal
- Office rent (or home office %)
- Utilities (business %)
- Software and subscriptions
- Professional services (accountancy, legal)
- Employee wages
- Vehicle (mileage at HMRC rates)
- Travel and accommodation
- Marketing and advertising
- Insurance
- Equipment (depreciated as capital allowance)

### Trading Allowance
- £1,000 annual allowance for small businesses
- No need to itemize expenses
- Or claim actual expenses if >£1,000

---

## VAT (Sales Tax)

**Rate**: 20% standard (0% and 5% on some items)

### VAT Registration
- Threshold: £90,000 (2024)
- Must register if exceeds threshold
- Can register voluntarily if below

### VAT Returns
- Quarterly (or monthly for large traders)
- Claim input VAT (tax paid on purchases)
- Pay difference to HMRC

**Example**:
- Sales with VAT: £120,000 (£100k + £20k VAT)
- Purchases with VAT: £60,000 (£50k + £10k VAT)
- Net VAT owed: £10,000

---

## Corporate Tax

### Corporation Tax Rate
- 19% on profits up to £50,000
- 25% on profits over £250,000
- Marginal rate: 25% on profits £50-250k
- Allowance (profit with 0% tax): £50,000

### Dividend Tax
- Dividends from company: Taxed to shareholder
- Dividend tax rates: 8.75-39.35% depending on income
- Dividend allowance: £1,000 (tax-free)

---

## Compliance & Deadlines

### Tax Return (Self-Assessment)
- Sole trader: January 31 following tax year
- Quarterly estimated payments not required (pay annually)

### Companies House Filing
- Accounts filed: 9 months after year-end (private co)
- Public company: 4 months after year-end

### VAT Returns
- Quarterly: Due within 1 month of quarter-end
- All payments now via Making Tax Digital (online system)

### PAYE (Payroll)
- If paying employees: Monthly deductions
- Deadline: 14th of following month

---

## Capital Allowances (Depreciation)

### Plant & Machinery
- 18% per year (reducing balance)
- Or 100% first-year allowance for small/medium businesses on some assets

### Structures and Buildings
- 3% per year (4% after April 2023)
- Building must be for commercial use

### Vehicles
- Cars: 18% reducing balance
- Vans: Allowance available
- Motorcycles: Full write-off in year 1

---

## Unique UK Benefits

### Annual Investment Allowance (AIA)
- £1,000,000 per year
- Can claim 100% first-year deduction on plant and machinery
- Helps with equipment purchases

### Research & Development Tax Credit
- Claim relief for R&D costs
- Up to 33% tax relief for loss-making companies
- Valuable for tech and innovation companies

### Trading Losses
- Can offset against other income or future years
- Can carry back 3 years (post-April 2023) or forward indefinitely

---

## National Insurance (Payroll Tax)

### Self-Employed
- Class 2: £163.80/year (fixed, if profit >£6,725)
- Class 4: 8-9% on profits £12,570-50,270, then 2% above

**Example**: £100k profit:
- Class 2: £164
- Class 4: £3,779 (8% on £47,270)
- Total NI: ~£3,943

## Common Mistakes

### ❌ Ignoring VAT Threshold
- Reach £90k revenue and forget to register
- Backdated VAT bill + penalties

### ❌ Not Maximizing Allowances
- Trading allowance (£1,000) or actual expenses?
- Annual investment allowance (£1m) on equipment
- Dividend allowance (£1,000)

### ❌ Poor Record Keeping
- Making Tax Digital requires digital records
- Keep for 6 years minimum
- Penalties for poor records

### ❌ Missing Deadlines
- SA return (Jan 31)
- Quarterly VAT
- Companies House filing
- PAYE (14th of month)

---

## Financial Management

### Bookkeeping
- Monthly reconciliation essential for VAT
- Keep records digitally (MTD requirement)
- Track allowable expenses

### Year-End (April 5)
- Most tax year ends April 5
- File tax return by January 31 following year
- Pay tax by January 31

## UK Financial Wisdom

**Limited Company vs. Sole Trader**:
- Both can achieve similar tax results
- Ltd company offers: Limited liability, professional image, easier to sell
- Sole trader: Simpler, lower admin cost

**Plan structure for your goals**, not just current year taxes.
