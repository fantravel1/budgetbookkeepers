# Australia: Tax & Business Guide

## Tax System Overview

### Income Tax Brackets (2024)
- 0%: $0-18,200 (tax-free threshold)
- 19%: $18,201-45,000
- 32.5%: $45,001-120,000
- 37%: $120,001-180,000
- 45%: $180,001+
- Medicare Levy: 2% on top (healthcare)

### Capital Gains
- 50% concession for individuals (50% of gain included in income)
- Taxed at marginal rate
- CGT discount: Hold 12+ months for 50% discount

## Business Structures

### Sole Trader
- Simplest structure
- Tax at personal rates (19-47%)
- No liability protection
- Minimal compliance

### Partnership
- Two or more people
- Each partner taxed on their share
- Pass-through entity
- Joint and several liability

### Company
- Limited liability
- Corporate tax rate: 30% (25% if small business <$50M turnover)
- More compliance
- Can distribute profits as dividends

### Trust (Family or Discretionary)
- Used for asset protection and income splitting
- Trustee taxed on profits
- Beneficiaries taxed on distributions
- Complex, needs professional setup

---

## Key Deductions

### Universal Allowable Expenses
- Office rent (or home office %)
- Utilities
- Office supplies
- Professional services
- Vehicle expenses (mileage at ATO rate or actual)
- Travel for business
- Marketing and advertising
- Insurance
- Equipment (depreciated as capital allowance)
- Software and subscriptions

### Home Office
- Can claim as % of home expenses
- Or flat rate: $17.50/hour worked at home (post-COVID allowance)

---

## GST (Goods & Services Tax)

**Rate**: 10% standard

### GST Registration
- Threshold: $75,000 annual turnover
- Must register if exceed threshold
- Can register voluntarily below

### GST Returns
- Monthly, quarterly, or annually depending on registration
- Claim input tax credits (GST paid on expenses)
- Remit difference to ATO

---

## Corporate Tax

### Company Tax Rate
- 30% (most companies)
- 25% (if small business, <$50M turnover)
- Future changes possible (may increase to 33%)

### Franking Credits
- Dividends include franking credits
- Shareholder gets credit for tax paid by company
- Reduces double taxation

### Small Business Concessions
- Lower company tax rate: 25% (vs. 30%)
- Simplified depreciation
- Better cash flow management rules

---

## Depreciation (Capital Allowances)

### Instant Asset Depreciation Allowance (2024)
- Assets <$20,000: 100% deduction
- Up to $5M per year eligible
- Accelerated deduction, good for cash flow

### Depreciating Assets (20+ years)
- Standard depreciation: 5-40% depending on asset type
- Building: 2.5% per year (slow)
- Equipment: 5-40% per year

---

## Superannuation (Retirement)

### Compulsory Contributions (Employer)
- 11.5% of salary (going to 12% 2025)
- Paid into employee superannuation
- Tax deductible by employer

### Self-Employed
- No compulsory contribution
- Voluntary contributions to super
- Tax deductible contributions
- Concessional rate: 15% tax on contributions (vs. marginal rate 47%)

---

## Compliance & Deadlines

### Individual Tax Return
- Due: October 31 each year (extended to November 30 if using tax agent)

### Company Tax Return
- Due: 5 months after year-end
- June 30 year-end: Due Nov 30

### PAYG Withholding
- If paying employees: Weekly/monthly withholding
- Quarterly Super: By 28th of month following quarter

### GST Returns
- Monthly, quarterly, or annual based on registration
- Generally due 1 month after lodgement deadline

---

## Unique Australian Features

### Division 7A (Loans & Distributions)
- Companies lending to shareholders: Deemed dividend
- Loans above $5,000 must comply or taxed as distribution
- Complex rules, needs professional advice

### Work-Related Expense Deductions
- Home-based workers: $17.50/hour from home (2022 onwards)
- Car expenses: Cents-per-km or actual
- Meals & entertainment: Generally not deductible

### Tax Losses
- Can carry forward indefinitely (if company)
- Can potentially carry back 1 year (certain conditions)
- Limited by continuity of ownership rules

## Common Mistakes

### ❌ Ignoring GST Threshold
- Hit $75k turnover, forget to register
- Backdated registration required

### ❌ Not Using Instant Asset Allowance
- <$20k equipment: Claim 100% year 1
- Don't spread depreciation unnecessarily

### ❌ Ignoring Superannuation
- Missing concessional tax rate on contributions
- Self-employed should contribute ($27,500 limit)

### ❌ Poor Division 7A Compliance
- Loans to shareholders not documented properly
- Deemed dividends trigger unexpected tax

## Financial Management

### Year-End: June 30
- Most businesses use June 30 year-end
- Tax return due Nov 30 (if using agent)

### Quarterly Super Payment
- Due 28th of month following quarter
- Q1 (July-Sep): Due Oct 28
- Q2 (Oct-Dec): Due Jan 28
- Q3 (Jan-Mar): Due Apr 28
- Q4 (Apr-Jun): Due Jul 28

## Australia Financial Wisdom

**Two major tax advantages:**
1. **Capital gains concession**: 50% of gains not taxed (plan asset sales)
2. **Superannuation**: 15% concessional tax on contributions (vs. 47% personal)

Structure with these in mind for significant tax savings.
