# India: Tax & Business Guide

## Tax System Overview

### Corporate Tax
- Standard rate: 30%
- Plus surcharge: Up to 12% (total ~34%)
- Plus cess: 4% (total ~37%)
- Effective: ~34-37% on corporate profits

### Individual Income Tax
- 0-30% depending on income
- Plus surcharge and cess
- Effective rates similar to corporate

### GST (Goods & Services Tax)
- Standard: 18% (most goods/services)
- Reduced: 5%, 12% (certain items)
- Exempted: Agricultural, medical, etc.

---

## Business Structures

### Sole Proprietorship
- Simplest structure
- Income taxed at personal rates (0-30%+)
- Unlimited liability
- Registration: Basic (UDYAM registration for MSMEs)

### Partnership
- 2+ persons
- Partnership deed required
- Each partner taxed on share
- Unlimited liability (typically)

### Limited Liability Company (Pvt Ltd or Ltd)
- Corporate tax: 30-37% effective
- Limited liability
- Professional structure
- More compliance (annual returns, audit)

### One Person Company (OPC)
- Single shareholder
- Limited liability
- Good for solopreneurs
- Higher compliance than sole proprietorship

---

## Key Deductions

### Universal Expenses
- Office rent (or home office %)
- Utilities
- Office supplies
- Professional services
- Vehicle expenses
- Travel for business
- Marketing
- Insurance
- Equipment (depreciated 15-40% per year)
- Employee salaries (major deduction)
- Donations (50% deductible to charity)

### Important Deductions
- Interest on borrowed capital
- Depreciation on equipment
- Bad debts (if proved)
- Medical insurance premiums (self and family)

---

## Depreciation (Capital Allowance)

### Straight-Line Method
- Standard rates vary by asset type
- Building: 5% per year
- Plant & machinery: 15% per year
- Furniture & fittings: 10% per year
- Motor vehicles: 15-20% per year
- IT equipment: 40% per year

### Accelerated Depreciation
- 40% on plant & machinery in first year
- Benefit of accelerated cash flow

---

## Tax Incentives

### Startup Deduction
- 3-year exemption from corporate tax (certain startups)
- Requirements: Registered before specific date
- Declining: 50% for year 4-5, 25% for year 6-7

### MSMEs (Micro, Small, Medium Enterprises)
- Lower tax rates (if turnover <₹400 crore)
- Various deductions and benefits
- Government support programs

### Export Incentives
- Export income may qualify for benefits
- SEIS (Service Export from India Scheme)
- Varies by sector

---

## GST Compliance

### GST Registration
- Threshold: ₹40 lakh turnover (₹20 lakh for NE states)
- Must register if exceed
- Monthly returns

### GST Returns
- Monthly: Form GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B
- Annual: Form GSTR-9
- Input tax credits available

---

## Compliance & Deadlines

### Corporate Tax Return
- Due: 30th day after audit (typically Sept 30 for Dec 31 year-end)

### GST Return
- GSTR-1: 11th of following month (suppliers)
- GSTR-3B: 20th of following month (payment)

### Annual Financial Statements
- If turnover >₹1 crore: Audit required
- Financial statements filed within 30 days of audit

### TDS (Tax Deducted at Source)
- Monthly payment required
- Due: 7th of following month

---

## Payroll (Deductions & Compliance)

### Employee Contributions
- Income tax withholding (0-30%+)
- Provident fund: 12% (employee) + 12% (employer)
- ESI: 0.75% (employee) + 3.25% (employer) - if payroll >$20k/month

### Employer Obligations
- File quarterly returns (Form 24Q)
- Annual summary (Form 27EQ)
- Deadline: 7th of following month (TDS)

---

## Common Mistakes

### ❌ Poor Documentation
- Indian tax authority very particular about documentation
- Every expense must have receipt
- Without proof, deduction denied

### ❌ Not Registering for GST
- Reach ₹40 lakh, fail to register
- Backdated registration with penalties

### ❌ Ignoring TDS Obligations
- If paying contractors, must deduct TDS
- Non-compliance = severe penalties

### ❌ Valuation Issues
- Transfer pricing scrutiny on related-party transactions
- Must document arm's length pricing

## Structure Comparison

**Sole Prop vs. Company**:
- Sole prop: 0-30%+ personal tax, simpler, less compliance
- Company: 30-37% corporate tax, more compliance, limited liability
- Decision depends on profit level and liability concerns
