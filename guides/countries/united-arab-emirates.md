# United Arab Emirates: Tax & Business Guide

## Tax System Overview

### Corporate Tax (NEW as of Jan 1, 2023)
- 0%: Up to AED 375,000 (~$102k USD) annual profit
- 15%: Above AED 375,000
- Transition: 9% for year 1 if applicable

**Note**: Previously 0% corporate tax. New rate applies 2023 onwards.

### Personal Income Tax
- **0%**: United Arab Emirates has NO personal income tax
- One of world's most tax-friendly jurisdictions

### Value Added Tax (VAT)
- Rate: 5% (low by global standards)
- Threshold: AED 375,000 (~$102k)

---

## Key Advantage: No Personal Income Tax

Unlike most countries, UAE offers:
- **Zero income tax** on personal earnings
- **Zero income tax** on business profits (under profit threshold)
- Huge draw for entrepreneurs and employees
- Salary + business income tax-free

---

## Business Structures

### Free Zone Company (Most Popular)
- Established in free zones (DIFC, JAFZA, etc.)
- **0% tax** on profits (regardless of amount)
- **0% import/export tariffs**
- **100% foreign ownership** allowed
- Costs: Initial setup ($500-2,000) + annual fees ($1,500-3,000)

**Advantage**: Complete tax exemption for strategic businesses.

### Mainland Company (Limited)
- Requires local partner (unless specific sectors)
- **15% tax** on profits above AED 375,000
- More restrictions
- Fewer advantages than free zone

### Sole Establishment/Professional License
- Professional services (consulting, etc.)
- Can be personal and professional combined
- Simpler than company formation
- 0% or 15% tax depending on emirate

---

## VAT (Value Added Tax)

**Standard rate**: 5% (lowest in region)

### VAT Registration
- Threshold: AED 375,000 (~$102,000) annual revenue
- Can register voluntarily below threshold
- Quarterly or monthly returns depending on business

### VAT Returns
- Quarterly (or monthly if requested)
- Due 28 days after quarter-end
- Input VAT credits available

---

## Deductions & Expenses

### Universal Deductible Expenses
- Office rent
- Utilities (business %)
- Office supplies
- Professional services
- Insurance
- Equipment (depreciated)
- Marketing and advertising
- Travel for business
- Vehicle expenses (if business use documented)
- Employee salaries (major deduction)

**Important**: Good documentation required (receipts, invoices).

---

## Depreciation (Capital Allowance)

### General Depreciation Rates
- Building: 5% per year
- Equipment: 10-33% per year depending on type
- Vehicles: 20-33% per year
- IT equipment: 33% per year

## Corporate Structure Advantages

### Free Zone Benefits
1. **0% corporate tax** (vs. 15% mainland)
2. **0% import/export duties**
3. **100% foreign ownership** (vs. 49% mainland limit)
4. **Lease office vs. invest in property**
5. **Simplified labor rules** in some zones

### Mainland Limited Companies
- Profit tax: 15% (if profit > AED 375,000)
- Requires local partner (51% ownership usually)
- More restrictions but larger market access

**Most choice**: Free zone company for tax optimization, mainland for market access.

---

## Employee Compensation

### Salaries (Not taxed)
- Employees pay 0% income tax
- Attractive for hiring top talent
- No withholding required

### Labor Law Compliance
- Private sector law applies
- Annual leave: 30 days
- End of service benefit: Proportional
- Gratuity: Required

---

## Compliance & Deadlines

### Corporate Tax Return
- Due: 4 months after fiscal year-end
- Generally Dec 31 year-end: Due April 30

### VAT Return
- Quarterly: Due 28 days after quarter-end
- Generally: End of month following quarter + 28 days

### Annual Report & Financial Statements
- Due: 4 months after year-end
- Audit required (if turnover >AED 2.5M or per company bylaws)

## Common Mistakes

### ❌ Ignoring VAT Threshold
- Reach AED 375,000, forget to register
- Backdated registration required
- Penalties apply

### ❌ Choosing Mainland Instead of Free Zone
- Missing 15% corporate tax savings
- Free zone 0% tax is major advantage
- Setup cost ($500-2k) paid back in tax savings quickly

### ❌ Poor Documentation
- Authorities strict on documentation
- Every transaction must be evidenced
- Without receipts, expenses denied

### ❌ Mixing Personal & Business
- Can claim home office but must document
- Keep business and personal separated
