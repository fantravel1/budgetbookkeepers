Industry-Specific Bookkeeping Guides

Tailored accounting guidance for your specific business type

Why Industry Matters for Bookkeeping

While basic bookkeeping principles are universal, different industries have unique accounting challenges. An e-commerce business needs inventory tracking. A salon needs commission management. A nonprofit needs grant tracking. Hiring a bookkeeper who understands your industry can save you thousands and help you make better business decisions.

üõí E-commerce & Online Retail

Unique Accounting Challenges

  • Inventory Tracking: COGS (cost of goods sold) calculations. Different inventory valuation methods (FIFO, LIFO, average cost) affect profit calculations.
  • Multiple Payment Processors: Income from Shopify, WooCommerce, Stripe, PayPal, Amazon Pay, etc. needs to be reconciled and categorized correctly.
  • Sales Tax Nexus: Complex rules about which states/countries you owe sales tax in, especially post-Wayfair ruling.
  • Returns & Refunds: High return rates can distort profit calculations if not handled correctly.
  • Shipping Costs: Are they COGS or operating expenses? The answer affects your profit picture.
  • Platform Fees: Shopify fees, marketplace fees (Amazon, eBay), payment processing fees all affect net profit.

What to Look For in an E-commerce Bookkeeper

  • Experience with your specific platform (Shopify, WooCommerce, Amazon, Etsy, etc.)
  • Knowledge of inventory accounting and COGS calculation
  • Understanding of sales tax rules for online selling
  • Ability to integrate with your e-commerce platform's accounting software

Key Questions to Ask

  • "How do you handle inventory? What method do you use?"
  • "Can you integrate with my Shopify/WooCommerce/Amazon account directly, or do you need manual data entry?"
  • "How do you track sales tax by jurisdiction?"
  • "How often do you reconcile each payment processor?"

üíº Freelance & Professional Services

Unique Accounting Challenges

  • Invoice Tracking: Often have many clients with different payment terms. Need to track who owes what and when it's due.
  • Inconsistent Income: Some months are feast, others are famine. Requires good cash flow planning.
  • Multiple Income Sources: May earn from different clients, platforms (Upwork, Fiverr, Toptal), retainers, and project work.
  • Business vs. Personal Expenses: Home office, equipment, software subscriptions all require careful categorization.
  • Quarterly Estimated Taxes: As a sole proprietor, you need to pay quarterly estimated taxes based on projected income.
  • 1099s & Reporting: Clients send you 1099s (or international equivalents). Must match with your records.

What to Look For

  • Experience with freelancers and multiple income sources
  • Understanding of home office deductions
  • Knowledge of quarterly estimated tax requirements
  • Ability to track invoices and accounts receivable

Key Questions

  • "How do you handle multiple clients and invoice tracking?"
  • "Can you help me understand quarterly estimated tax payments?"
  • "How do you categorize home office expenses?"
  • "What's your experience with 1099 tracking?"

üçï Food & Beverage (Restaurants, Cafes, Catering)

Unique Accounting Challenges

  • Inventory Management: Food costs are your largest expense. Daily usage, spoilage, and waste tracking is critical.
  • Labor Costs: Often 25-35% of revenue. Need to track hours, overtime, tips, payroll taxes carefully.
  • POS System Integration: Most restaurants use Square, Toast, or other POS systems. Must integrate with accounting software.
  • Tips Tracking: Complex tax implications. Must track cash tips, credit card tips, and credit card tips on tips.
  • Food Cost Percentages: Industry benchmarks are critical. Know your ideal food cost % and track actual vs. ideal.
  • Licenses & Permits: Liquor licenses, health permits ‚Äî all have annual/quarterly renewal costs that affect accounting.
  • Accrued Expenses: Cleaning, pest control, and other recurring expenses.

What to Look For

  • Specific experience with food & beverage accounting
  • Knowledge of inventory tracking for restaurants
  • Understanding of tip tax implications
  • Ability to integrate with your POS system
  • Food cost analysis expertise

Key Questions

  • "What's your experience with inventory tracking in restaurants?"
  • "How do you calculate and track food cost percentages?"
  • "Can you integrate with our POS system directly?"
  • "How do you handle tip reporting and payroll taxes on tips?"

üíÖ Beauty & Wellness (Salons, Spas, Fitness)

Unique Accounting Challenges

  • Commission Tracking: Stylists, therapists, or trainers typically work on commission (30-50% of service revenue). Must track commissions accurately.
  • Multiple Service Lines: Different services have different profit margins. A $50 haircut vs. $150 color ‚Äî need to track profitability by service.
  • Retail Sales: Many salons sell products (shampoo, oils, etc.). Retail inventory, margins, and sales mix are different from services.
  • Tips: Significant portion of income. Must track and handle for payroll purposes.
  • Membership Dues/Packages: Pre-paid packages or memberships need to be recognized as revenue when earned, not when sold.
  • Supplier Payments: Frequent purchases of supplies, products, chemicals. Need category tracking.

What to Look For

  • Experience with commission tracking and payroll
  • Understanding of service business accounting
  • Ability to track profitability by service type
  • Knowledge of membership/package revenue recognition
  • Experience with salon/spa POS systems

Key Questions

  • "How do you calculate and track stylist/therapist commissions?"
  • "Can you show me profitability by service (haircuts vs. color vs. waxing, etc.)?"
  • "How do you handle deferred revenue from pre-paid packages?"
  • "What's your experience with [your specific POS system]?"

ü§ù Nonprofits & Community Organizations

Unique Accounting Challenges

  • Fund Accounting: Different funds (restricted, unrestricted, quasi-endowment) with different spending rules.
  • Grant Tracking: Each grant has its own budget, timeline, and requirements. Must track spending against each grant.
  • Donor Restrictions: Donations often come with restrictions (e.g., "for arts programs only"). Must track and enforce.
  • Form 990 Preparation: Annual IRS filing (Form 990, 990-N, or 990-EZ depending on size). Complex and detailed.
  • Audit Requirements: Organizations over $500K revenue usually need annual independent audits.
  • In-Kind Donations: Donated goods/services must be valued and tracked (not just cash donations).
  • Board Reporting: Boards need specific financial reports that for-profits don't.

What to Look For

  • Nonprofit-specific accounting experience (not just general bookkeeping)
  • Knowledge of fund accounting
  • Experience with grant tracking
  • Understanding of Form 990 preparation
  • Familiarity with nonprofit financial reporting standards

Key Questions

  • "What's your experience with nonprofit fund accounting?"
  • "Can you help us track grants and ensure we're spending within restrictions?"
  • "How do you help prepare for Form 990?"
  • "What financial reports do you prepare for the board?"

üè† Real Estate (Agents, Property Managers, Short-term Rentals)

Unique Accounting Challenges

  • Commission Tracking: Real estate agents earn commission on sales (percentage of sale price). Complex split tracking between agents and brokers.
  • Rental Income & Expenses: For property managers or Airbnb owners, rental income, property taxes, maintenance, vacancy rates all affect profitability.
  • Depreciation: Real estate (buildings, improvements) can be depreciated over 27.5-39 years, creating significant tax deductions.
  • Capital Improvements vs. Repairs: Replacing a roof (capital improvement) is treated differently than fixing a leaky faucet (repair).
  • Escrow Accounting: Agents/brokers handle client money in escrow accounts. Strict accounting rules apply.
  • 1099 Tracking: Commission structures mean frequent 1099 issuances to agents or contractors.

What to Look For

  • Real estate-specific accounting experience
  • Understanding of commission tracking
  • Knowledge of depreciation rules
  • Experience with rental property accounting
  • Familiarity with escrow accounting (if applicable)

Key Questions

  • "What's your experience with real estate commission tracking?"
  • "How do you track depreciation on properties?"
  • "Can you help identify capital improvements vs. repairs?"
  • "How do you handle escrow accounting?" (if applicable)

The Bottom Line

Your industry's unique characteristics require a bookkeeper who understands those characteristics. Don't settle for a generalist if your industry needs a specialist. A bookkeeper experienced in your industry will:

  • Understand your industry's key metrics and profit drivers
  • Catch accounting mistakes that generalists might miss
  • Provide industry-specific insights that help you make better decisions
  • Know common pitfalls and how to avoid them
  • Be able to integrate with your industry's standard software
